Complexity Considerations

  • Tesler's Law: The Law of Conservation of Complexity
    • For any system, there is a certain amount of complexity that cannot be reduced.
  • "As Easy as Email":
    • Allow users to learn by doing instead of tutorials
    • Help users adapt more efficient methods - in-context learning, tooltips, etc.
    • Provide fluid pathways
    • Reduce clutter without losing complexity
    • Make important information visually salient

Emotional/Accessibility Principles

Human Centered Design Principles

  • Puts human needs, capabilities, and behavior first
  • Design to meet those needs

Affordances vs Signifiers

  • Affordance: a clue that an action can be performed
  • Signifier: a guide/direction to help users use an affordance

The Seven Stages of Action

  1. What do I want to accomplish?
  2. What are the alternative action sequences?
  3. What action can I take now?
  5. What happened?
  7. Is this OK?
  • Provide discoverability
  • Provide feedback
  • Utilize a conceptual model
  • Include affordances, signifiers and constraints
  • Ensure you have good mappings - the relationship between controls and their actions

Cultural/Gender Considerations

  • The Seven Dimensions of Culture:
    1. Individualism vs communitarianism
    2. Universalism vs peculiarism
    3. Specific vs diffuse
    4. Neutral vs emotional
    5. Achievement vs absorption
    6. Sequential time vs synchronous
    7. Internal vs outer direction
